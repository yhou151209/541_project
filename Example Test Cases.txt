1.Unavailable
  Scenario:
	Ian becomes unavailable for Sunday evening.
  Expected behavior:
	Ian is removed from that shift
	Replacement staff are assigned
	Explanation mentions:
		Unavailability
		Replacement reasoning

 
2.Direct Swap
  Scenario:
	Brian (Monday evening) swaps with Ian (Tuesday evening)
  Expected behavior:
	Their assignments are exchanged
	Additional adjustments may occur to maintain feasibility
	Explanation confirms:
		Swap validity
		Constraint satisfaction

3.Avoid Back-to-Back
  Scenario:
	Avoid assigning Eric to both morning and evening on the same day
  Expected behavior:
	Preference is added to the system
	Schedule may or may not change depending on feasibility
	Explanation reports:
		Before vs after count of back-to-back assignments


4.Avoid Shift Type
  Scenario:
	Try not to assign Alice to morning shifts
  Expected behavior:
	Preference is stored
	Morning assignments for Alice are reduced if possible
	Explanation reflects:
		Change (or no change) in assignment count


5.Avoid Specific Day
  Scenario:
	Avoid assigning Hannah on Sunday
  Expected behavior:
	System attempts to reduce Sunday assignments
	If not feasible, schedule remains unchanged
	Explanation clarifies trade-offs


6.Avoid Specific Day + Shift
  Scenario:
	Avoid assigning Brian to Saturday evening
  Expected behavior:
	System tries to reassign that slot
	Busy shift logic may conflict with preference
	Explanation highlights:
		Preference vs operational constraints


7.Busy Shift Behavior
  Scenario:
	Triggered implicitly (e.g., after unavailable or swap)
  Expected behavior:
	Full-time staff are preferred for:
		Evening shifts
		Weekend shifts
	Explanation includes:
		“Busy shifts prefer full-time staff when possible”


8.Preference Accumulation
  Scenario:
	Apply multiple preferences sequentially
  Expected behavior:
	Preferences are stored and accumulated
	Future schedules consider all preferences together
